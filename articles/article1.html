Wabi-Sabi & Imperfection

Wabi-sabi teaches that cracks are not flaws but records of survival. The world does not become beautiful through perfection — it becomes beautiful through time.

In a hyper-optimized digital culture, quiet imperfection feels human. A worn notebook, fading ink, old wood, unfinished thoughts — they remind us that life is becoming, not performing.

Maybe healing is not about returning to who we were. Maybe it is about learning to admire the new shape of ourselves.
